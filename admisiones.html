Overview

Replacing paper enrollment — without losing what works

Eccole is a startup building software for private K-12 schools in LATAM. Their first bet: digitize school admissions. The problem was that admissions at private schools in the region is deeply relational — it's not just a form, it's a conversation with the school director, references from parents, and a physical tour.

My role was to run discovery — understand what "admissions" really means for schools and parents — and then design an MVP that digitizes the right parts without stripping out the relationship layer that makes private school admissions work.

Starting Point

No product existed. No designs. Just a hypothesis: "schools want to digitize admissions." My job was to find out if that was true, and if so, which parts.

01 — Discovery

What does "admissions" actually mean?

I ran a two-week discovery sprint: 8 interviews with school administrators, 6 with parents who had recently enrolled a child, and a workflow audit of the physical admissions process at 3 schools.

The key insight: schools don't want to eliminate paper — they want to eliminate the follow-up. The most painful part of admissions is tracking which families have submitted which documents, and chasing them down. That's the problem worth solving.

02 — Definition

Scoping the MVP — what to build first

With the discovery findings, I ran a prioritization session with the Eccole founders. We mapped features against impact (what reduces the most pain) and effort (what can we build in 8 weeks). The MVP scope emerged: a digital application form, document upload, and an admin tracking dashboard.

03 — Prototype & Testing

Testing assumptions before building

I built a clickable prototype in Figma and ran two rounds of usability testing — one with school administrators, one with parents. The biggest surprise from testing: parents expected to upload photos of physical documents (not fill out digital forms). The flow had to accommodate both mental models.

04 — MVP Design

The final product

The MVP launched with two views: a parent-facing web form for submitting the application and documents, and an admin dashboard for tracking application status and sending follow-up nudges. The design prioritized mobile-web for parents (most accessed on phone) and desktop for administrators.
